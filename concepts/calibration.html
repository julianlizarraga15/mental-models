Concept

Calibration

Calibration means a model's confidence should match how often it is actually right.

Bad calibration
Model says 90% confidence, but is right only 60% of the time.
Good calibration
Among predictions with 90% confidence, about 90% are correct.
Temperature scaling
A common post-training method that softens or sharpens probabilities.

Simple mental model

Accuracy asks: “is the model right?” Calibration asks: “does the model know how likely it is to be right?”

Why it matters

Calibration matters when decisions depend on confidence: triage, thresholds, ranking, human review, risk scoring, or abstaining when uncertain.
