Core model

Rule

Broker is not the main variable.

IOL, PPI, PPI Global or IBKR change access and commissions. The tax treatment mostly follows the instrument.

Best case

Same exposure + liquid CEDEAR available → usually prefer CEDEAR.

Local sale is generally more tax efficient than selling a foreign ETF directly.

Fallback

No good CEDEAR equivalent → Irish accumulating ETF.

Cleaner product than USA direct ETFs for non-US investors, but Argentine capital gains still matter.

Ranking: same underlying exposure

1

Liquid ETF CEDEAR

SPY, IVV, QQQ, EEM, IWM

Best Argentine tax treatment; watch spread/liquidity.

2

Irish accumulating ETF

IWDA, CSPX, VUAA, VWRA

Good structure; sale usually pays 15% on realized gain.

3

Irish distributing ETF

VUSA, VWRD

Like Irish accumulating, but also pays cash dividends.

4

USA ETF direct

VOO, VTI, VT, SPY

Usually worst for Argentina: capital gains + dividend withholding.

5

Illiquid CEDEAR

thin order book / wide spread

Tax-efficient, but the spread can eat the advantage.

Examples

S&P 500

World exposure

No perfect VT-style CEDEAR? Use SPY + VEA + EEM, or accept IWDA/VWRA abroad.

EEM adds emerging markets. IWM adds US small caps. GLD adds gold, not equities.
